Bandar Lampung

(n.) the capital of the province of Lampung and is one of the largest and most populous cities outside the island of Java

History The history of the city of

Bandar Lampung

Before becoming Bandar Lampung City, this area was a combination of two twin cities, namely Tanjung Karang City and Teluk Betung City. The two twin cities were previously part of South Lampung Regency. After the issuance of Law No. 22 of 1948, the two cities were separated from South Lampung Regency, and were introduced as Tanjung Karang-Teluk Betung City. Furthermore, the status of Tanjungkarang City and Teluk Betung City changed and underwent several expansions. Until 1965, the Lampung Residency was upgraded to Lampung Province based on Law Number 18 of 1965, and the Tanjungkarang-Telukbetung City was changed to the Tanjungkarang-Telukbetung Level II Regional Municipality and at the same time became the capital of Lampung Province.

Then based on Government Regulation No. 24 of 1983, the Tanjungkarang-Telukbetung Level II Municipal Municipality was changed to the Level II Municipal Municipality of Bandar Lampung. Furthermore, based on the Decree of the Minister of Home Affairs Number 43 of 1998 concerning changes in the official text structure within the District/Municipal Governments of Level II Regions throughout Indonesia which was then followed up by the Decree of the Mayor of Bandar Lampung Number 17 of 1999 there was a change in the mention of the name from "Level II Regional Municipal Government". Bandar Lampung” became “Bandar Lampung City Government” which is still in use today.
